Lamb Shank

Description

Lamb shank is a cut of meat from the lower leg of a lamb, prized for its rich flavor and succulent texture when slow-cooked. The cut is naturally tough due to connective tissue, but this breaks down during long, slow cooking methods like braising, resulting in a tender, melt-in-your-mouth result. The bone adds flavor and richness, making it a popular choice for hearty, comforting meals.
